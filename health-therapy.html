Healthy Living

Physical Health & Wellness

A healthy body supports a healthy mind.

Upon or prior to admission to Willow House, we ensure that every young person is registered with a local GP, dentist, and optician. Confidential health records are meticulously maintained.

  • Nutritious Dining: Serving balanced, fresh meals prepared in-house, with children actively involved in meal selection and preparation.
  • Life Skills: Teaching children age-appropriate cooking, shopping, and hygiene independence skills.
  • Physical Activity: Encouraging active hobbies, sports, and outdoor activities in local clubs.

Meadows Psychology Service

Meadows Psychology Service is a clinical psychology-led team specializing in supporting residential children's settings through a trauma-informed lens. They provide:

  • Customised psychological formulations acting as roadmaps for care.
  • Targeted de-escalation strategies and regulation curve maps.
  • Direct clinical consultation to care teams.
Therapeutic Frameworks

Psychological Care & Formulations

We work closely with Meadows Psychology Service to understand the "why" behind behaviours, rather than just reacting to the behaviours themselves. Our therapeutic wrap-around support addresses:

  • EBD and developmental attachment trauma.
  • Risks of self-harm, substance misuse, or exploitation.
  • Additional neurodivergent diagnoses (e.g. ADHD, Autism).
Clinical Governance

Medication Safety

All prescribed and over-the-counter medications are administered strictly in accordance with prescription instructions by fully trained, competent staff:

  • Stored in a double-locked, secure steel cabinet inside the staff office.
  • Rigorous records maintained in individual logs.
  • Systematic audits conducted to guarantee clinical compliance.

Have questions about clinical safety?

Our registered manager can walk you through our medication audits and clinical governance guidelines.
